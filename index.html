Research

I have done research within both stellar and radio astronomy and astrophysics and am currently working on a project using gravitational waves for my undergraduate thesis.

Neutron Star Binaries

At the University of Toronto I am investigating a model to test the accuracy of the fit to a binary neutron star mass distribution for multiple populations of neutron star binaries. I am also currently investigating the different orbital properties of these different populations of neutron star binaries to see if there are any (in)consistencies.

Fast Radio Burst Counterpart Crossmatching

At McGill University as a CHIME collaboration member and Trottier Space Institute (TSI) summer student I worked on crossmatching Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) with other transients, more specifically Gamma-Ray Bursts (GRBs) and Gravitational Wave events. I developed python scripts that create and automatically update a catalog of FRBs, create and automatically update a catalog of GRB/GW transients, and crossmatch both of these catalogs. Then, I created real-time automated pipelines to do all of this on site at the Canadian Hydrogen Intensity Mapping Experiment (CHIME) and send an alert on the CHIME slack channel if there is a potential coincidence to be followed up by a collaboration member. I also followed up any potential coincidences over the summer in real time.

Long Secondary Periods in Red Giants

At the University of Toronto as a work study student I investigated the astrophysical cause of a property exhibited by about 1/3 of Red Giants known as the Long Secondary Period (LSP). I analyzed the patterns found in the light and phase curves for 11 stars with known LSPs from the American Association of Variable Star Observers (AAVSO). The inferred cause found was strong evidence to support the binary eclipse hypothesis. The Red Giant is theorized to be in a binary system with a low-mass dust-enshrouded companion and the companion eclipsing the Red Giant is causing this LSP we are seeing in our analysis. I also found strong evidence to support a theory that states this companion may not only be enshrouded in dust but have a dust tail as well.

Radio Astronomy Research Interface

At the University of Manitoba (virtually) I developed a prototype and coded the front end of a radio image research interface the Astronomy and Physics Department was developing. I designed and created the template for the interface in collaboration with Jayanne English. I also converted a prototype image from optical to radio for the interface, whose purpose was to convert optical images into the radio. This was all done using HTML, JavaScript and the GIMP software.
